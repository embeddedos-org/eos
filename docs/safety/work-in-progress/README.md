# Safety Documentation — Work in Progress

> ⚠️ **NOT A CERTIFIED SAFETY CASE.**
>
> This subtree contains skeleton templates for ISO 26262, IEC 61508, and DO‑178C
> safety lifecycle artifacts. The vast majority of fields are literal `[TODO]`
> placeholders. **No certification body would accept this as evidence today.**
>
> These templates exist so that an integrator pursuing functional safety
> certification of a derived product has a starting structure. Producing a
> real safety case is a multi-month engagement requiring:
>
> 1. A qualified functional safety engineer (e.g. TÜV-certified for ISO 26262 ASIL).
> 2. An accredited assessor (e.g. notified body for IEC 61508).
> 3. Tool qualification reports for every tool in the development pipeline.
> 4. Hazard analysis and risk assessment (HARA) against the specific item.
> 5. Verification and validation against the derived safety goals + ASIL/SIL/DAL.
>
> Until those steps are complete, **EmbeddedOS makes no safety claim**
> beyond the MIT-license disclaimers in `LICENSE`. See `SECURITY.md` for the
> separate vulnerability-disclosure process.
